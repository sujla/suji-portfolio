This project aimed to transform Olive Young’s static store finder into a map-based shopping tool.

The goal was to help customers connect product interest to nearby store availability and pickup, reducing friction between digital browsing and physical store purchase.

    Problem

    Olive Young had strong digital and physical store channels, but the journey between them was fragmented.

    When customers found a product online, they could not easily confirm whether it was available at a nearby store or eligible for pickup. The existing store finder was closer to a static store information page than a shopping tool, making store discovery, inventory checks, and pickup decisions feel fragmented.

    Why It Mattered to the Business

    • High-intent customers could drop off before purchase.
    • Store staff absorbed repetitive inventory-check calls during daily operations.

    Business Opportunity

    We saw the store finder as more than a utility feature.
    By redesigning it around store availability, product discovery, and pickup, it could become a business-driving touchpoint.

    Design Explorations

    I explored multiple design directions to translate the business opportunity and UX goal into practical product decisions. The explorations focused on three priorities: location-based discovery, clearer stock visibility, and a faster path to pickup.

    1. Map UX

    • How to make nearby stores visible at first glance
    • How to support both current-location search and manual store search
    • How to help users understand store distance and location context quickly

    2. Store List UX

    • How to translate map results into a scannable list for easier store comparison
    • How to help users recognize stores with storefront photos, not only store names
    • How to keep map and list states connected when users switch views

    3. Stock Availability

    • How to make store-level availability clear enough for purchase decisions
    • How to replace vague stock labels with more actionable information
    • Define empty, low-stock, and unavailable states so customers know the next best action.

    Final Solution

    Early Impact

    After launch, we looked at both product data and store feedback to understand whether the renewed store finder was helping customers discover nearby availability, move toward pickup, and reduce avoidable store inquiries.

    What we looked at

    • Available-store list page views
    • Pickup orders from the available-store list flow
    • Qualitative feedback from store staff

    Key Learnings

    The main value of this project was not only the initial uplift, but also the ability to start measuring cross-channel behavior more clearly.

    By connecting product interest, store availability, and pickup actions in one flow, the team gained a stronger foundation for future optimization based on real customer needs.

    It also became the foundation for the next improvement project, helping us identify additional UX problems users were facing further down the pickup journey.
