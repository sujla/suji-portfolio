Project Overview

@ CJ Olive Young, Inc. 2024

Contributions

    Platform

    • Mobile & PC Web

    Worked with

    • Product, Engineering, and Store Operations teams

    Problems

    Olive Young had strong digital and physical store businesses, but the connection between the two did not fully support how customers shop across channels.

    When customers found a product online, they could not easily confirm whether it was available at a nearby store or eligible for pickup. The existing store finder was closer to a static store information page than a shopping tool, making store discovery, inventory checks, and pickup decisions feel fragmented.

    Why It Mattered to the Business

    This created avoidable business friction that a better digital experience could have reduced:

    1. High-intent customers could drop off before purchase.

    2. Store staff absorbed repetitive inventory-check calls during daily operations.

    This fragmented experience created friction for two key user groups:

    Business Opportunity

    We saw the store finder as more than a utility feature.
    By redesigning it around store availability, product discovery, and pickup, it could become a business-driving touchpoint.

    Capture high-intent demand

    Help customers confirm nearby product availability before purchase intent drops.

    Turn availability into pickup

    Convert confirmed availability into a clear pickup order path.

    Reduce store inquiry load

    Reduce repetitive availability calls by making inventory easier to check digitally.

    UX Goal

    Create a map-based store finder that completes the shopping journey by connecting product interest to nearby store availability and pickup.

    Solutions

    To meet both the business and UX goals, we redesigned the store finder around three key improvements: location-based discovery, clearer inventory information, and a faster path to pickup.

    1.

    Map-first
    Store Discovery

    What Users Needed :

    Customers needed a faster way to find nearby stores based on their location.

    What We Changed :

    Replaced the static text-based store list with a map-first experience.

    Solution I

    Helped users quickly explore nearby stores or search for a specific store.

    2

    Store-level
    Stock Visibility

    What Users Needed :

    Customers wanted to know whether a product was available before visiting a store.

    What We Changed :

    Added store-by-store availability states directly into the product flow.

    Solution II

    Made nearby inventory easier to compare with clear stock and pickup labels.

    3

    Faster
    Pickup Decision

    What Users Needed :

    Shoppers needed confidence that the chosen store could complete their pickup order.

    What We Changed :

    Connected store selection, availability, and pickup actions in one decision flow.

    Solution III

    Reduced the path from discovery to pickup so users could order and visit with confidence.

    Early Impact

    After launch, we looked at both product data and store feedback to understand whether the renewed store finder was helping customers discover nearby availability, move toward pickup, and reduce avoidable store inquiries.

    What we looked at

    • Available-store list page views
    • Pickup orders from the available-store list flow
    • Qualitative feedback from store staff

    Key Learnings

    The main value of this project was not only the initial uplift, but also the ability to start measuring cross-channel behavior more clearly.

    By connecting product interest, store availability, and pickup actions in one flow, the team gained a stronger foundation for future optimization based on real customer needs.

    It also became the foundation for the next improvement project, helping us identify additional UX problems users were facing further down the pickup journey.
